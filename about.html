About the composer

brief bio

Clayton Simmons Davidson has been involved with different musical projects as a composer, guitarist, arranger and songwriter. His music has been performed around the United States and in Europe.

Mr. Davidson is interested in creating music that provides a visceral experience and which finds meaningful ways to reach out to audiences, including integrating new sounds, sound-worlds, and technologies into the experience. He is fascinated by tightly-integrated forms, polysemy, games, frames, and transcontextuality.

At this time, his compositional repertoire includes pieces written for a variety of instrumental ensembles including string quartet, woodwind quintet, percussion quartet and other mixed chamber ensembles, as well as solo instruments.

Mr. Davidson has studied composition under Andrew May, Michael Rothkopf, Lawrence Dillon, Kenneth Frazelle and Shane Monds.
